Railfence Technique

It is a example of transposition cipher. It is also known as zigzag cipher. It uses a simple algorithm. In this cipher plaintext is written downwards diagonally on successive “rails” of an fence, then moving up when the bottom rail is reached, moving down when the top rail is reached, and follow the same till the whole plaintext is written.And the cipher text is then read off in rows.

Example: Plaintext: all the best for exams

To encrypt this with a rail fence of depth two , we write the following

ciphered text: ALHBFRXMLTEEOEAS

It is very easy to break by the attacker
