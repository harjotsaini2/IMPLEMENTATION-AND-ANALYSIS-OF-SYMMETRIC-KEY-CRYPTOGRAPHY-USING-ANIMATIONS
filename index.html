LEARN CLASSICAL CIPHERED TECHNIQUES

Cryptography is defined as the study of secure communication techniques that allow the sender and intended receiver of a message to view its contents. The term is derived from the Greek word “kryptos” which means “hidden”. It is associated with encryption, which is the act of converting ordinary text into ciphertext and then back again upon arrival. In addition, cryptography also covers the hiding of information in images using various techniques.

Ancient Egyptians use these methods in hieroglyphics, and Roman Emperor Julius Caesar is credited with using one of the first modern ciphers.

The ciphertext is also known as encrypted or encoded information because it contains a form of the original plaintext that cannot be read by a human or computer without the key.


There are two types of cryptography

1. Symmetric key: It is a cryptographic technique where the sender and receiver of a message use a single same key to encrypt and decrypt the messages. Symmetric Key Systems are faster and simpler. The problem with symmetric key cryptography is that sender and receiver have to exchange the key securely.

2. Asymmetric key: It is a cryptographic technique in which a pair of keys is used to encrypt and decrypt information. A public key is used for encryption and a private key is used for decryption.

The public key is used to encode and a private key is used to decode the message. The private key is shared between the sender and receiver of the encrypted sensitive information.
