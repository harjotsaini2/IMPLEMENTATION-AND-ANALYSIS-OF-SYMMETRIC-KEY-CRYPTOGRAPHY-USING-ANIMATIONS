Shift Cipher

A shift cipher involves replacing each letter in the message by a letter that is some fixed number of positions further along in the alphabet. We’ll call this number the encryption key. It is just the length of the shift we are using. For example, upon encrypting the message “PHONE” using a shift cipher with encryption key 3, we obtain the encoded message (or ciphertext): SKRQI. One of the simplest ways to do this is with a shift cipher. Famously, Julius Caesar used this type of cipher when sending messages to his military commanders.

Formula for Encryption and decryption are the following:

Encryption= (Plain text + key)%26
Decryption= (Encrypted text - key)%26
